Changes made to the prototype

Functionality changes

We added a prototype setup page (ticket SFDE-117), so that your desired settings can be used before viewing the prototype. This includes:

  • release version (feature toggles)
  • the page to start the journey from (sign in, start page, businesses list or home page)
  • whether to trigger validation or not

With the ‘release version’ option, you can view the prototype as either Buffalo release 1 (MVP) or as ‘concept’. This means we can retain all our conceptual work in the prototype but still have the ability to view pages as we would like them built for the first release. This is achieved with a query string ?release=b1, ?release=concept. The Buffalo Release 1 (MVP) version was the version we put through user research.

We added a ‘Design spec’ link to each page (ticket SFDE-140). This contains metadata information for each page including page IDs, page names, validation errors, page titles, meta descriptions, URL slugs and links for dev tickets. Contents of these done as part of tickets SFDE-139, SFDE-143, SFDE-144.

Design changes

We prototyped the entirety of what is expected to be built for the first release (ticket SFDE-113).

MVP variants of the home and ‘your account’ pages were prototyped.

MVP home page includes:

  • a link to ‘Get funding to improve animal health and welfare’
  • a link to ‘View and update your business details’

MVP ‘Your account’ page includes:

  • a link to ‘View and update your personal details’
  • a link to ‘View a list of your businesses’

Other miscellaneous MVP pages/links include:

  • contact us
  • privacy
  • cookies (further work done on ticket SFDE-145)
  • accessibility statement
  • personal information charter (link to external page)
  • standard error pages (service is unavailable, there is a problem with the service or the page cannot be found)
  • signed out
  • auto signed out (after a 24 hour session)

Business and personal details

The ability to change personal and business details was based on both the existing Rural Payments service (RPS) and previous design work the single front door team had produced in the prototype kit and on Figma. We did not yet know the full business requirements for each of the data items, but we knew enough to prototype the bulk of the functionality. We replicated the existing RPS functionality, bringing it in line with GDS guidelines and design patterns.

Business details – we added a ‘Change’ journey for the following data items:

  • Business Name
  • Business Address
  • Business Telephone
  • Business Mobile
  • Business Email address
  • VAT registration number (also added an ‘Add’ journey for when it is blank)

We also made it possible to:

  • change legal status and business type online to test whether users would want to be able to do this
  • edit bank details online to test whether users would want to be able to do this – but the decision was then made to drop this from our MVP prototype. This is activated with the concept feature flag.

We also kept the view only display of Single business identifier (SBI) and Vendor registration number. We learnt that businesses may not have a vendor registration number, so prototyped a feature flag to hide this. Ticket SFDE-146.

We also learnt that businesses may have one, multiple or zero County Parish Holding (CPH) numbers. So we added all of these variants too. Ticket SFDE-146.

Personal details – we added a ‘Change’ journey for the following data items:

  • full name
  • date of birth
  • personal Address
  • personal Telephone
  • personal Mobile
  • personal Email address

We also kept the view only display of Customer reference number and Personal identifier.

Each page in the change and add flows has a ‘cancel’ link so users can return to the main details page without making changes.

On changing a data item, the user is returned to the full details page (business or personal) and a success notification banner is displayed.

Other prototype changes included:

  • removing the link to RPS from the beta banner as this does not align with the intended use of a beta banner and previously tested inconclusively. Designers proposed adding wording to the homepage to allow users to find their way back to RPS to do any tasks which are not going to be possible in our MVP, but the decision was made by Product to have no links to RPS in the MVP
  • minor style changes, for example to capitalisation (for example Single business identifier, not Single Business Identifier), and putting lists in alphabetical order
  • changing the header on each ‘update details’ page to a question, for example ‘What is your full name?’
  • making the personal name field a single field (viewable with the concept feature flag)
  • adding a provisional service level agreement of 2 days for RPS to respond to an email
  • drafting error messages for each field based on typical validation rules, as we did not yet know our specific validation rules
  • page URLs and metadata descriptions for each page in the prototype

We did not draft hint text yet to help users fill in fields on most pages, as we did not yet know what fields we would need to keep and what the validation rules would be.

When we are clearer on the requirements and validation rules, we will iterate the prototype again and add hint text if needed.

Assumptions tested, outcomes and decisions

  1. Most users will be able to update their business name successfully
  2. Most users will find the 'contact us' link in the footer of any page, click on it and be able to find out how to contact RPA for support
  3. Most users can navigate to both personal and business details
  4. Most users can successfully check and amend both personal and business details
  5. Most users only need to select one type of business
  6. Most users agree with the split between personal and business details
  7. Most users would find most or all fields relevant
  8. Most users do not feel any fields are missing
  9. Most users want to be able to complete as many details as possible (legal status, bank details) online rather than having to call RPA
  10. Most users can understand the business type options
  11. Most users have no need for an international address
  12. Most users are comfortable with providing data like phone number, address, email and believe it will be used for acceptable reasons (eg to contact them)
  13. Most users understand why they are asked to provide data in this service
  14. Most users understand updating their details in SFD will also update their details in the Rural Payments service
