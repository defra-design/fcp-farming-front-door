Introduction

1.1 The Annual Health and Welfare Review

The Annual Health and Welfare Review (“AHWR”) is a scheme run by the Rural Payments Agency (referred to below as “us, “we”, “our”) on behalf of the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (“Defra”). The AWHR is a financial assistance scheme under section 1 of the Agriculture Act 2020 and is in conjunction with the Agriculture (Financial Assistance) Regulations 2021 (“the Regulations”) amended by The Agriculture (Financial Assistance) (Amendment) Regulations 2022.

1.2 About these Terms and Conditions

  1. (a) The AHWR Beta 3 agreement (the “Agreement”) comprises these “Terms and Conditions”, which are applicable to the payment of the one-off sum (the “Payment”) to the agreement holder (“you”)
  2. (b) The AHWR scheme consists of multiple phases (also known as betas) and these Terms and Conditions only apply to beta 3 of the AHWR Private Beta (“Beta 3”) for a review of the health and welfare (“Review”) of a single herd or flock (a epidemiologically distinct unit of a single species) of beef cattle or dairy cattle, or sheep, or pigs (the “Single Species”).

3. A Review under Beta 3

3.1 You must ask a Royal College of Veterinary Surgeons (RCVS) registered vet (“Vet”) to complete a Review of the Single Species under Beta 3.

3.2 You must tell the Vet about the requirements for a Review under Beta 3, there is accompanying Beta 3 Vet guidance that will assist the Vet to carry out this AWHR. It is your responsibility to make sure that the Review completed by the Vet complies with these Terms and Conditions.

3.3 You must have the following minimum number of the relevant Single Species present on the date or dates the Vet carries out the Review:

  1. a. At least 11 dairy cattle.
  2. b. At least 11 beef cattle.
  3. c. At least 21 sheep.
  4. d. At least 51 pigs.

3.4 To complete a Review under Beta 3, you must ask the Vet to do all of the following:

  1. (a) visit the relevant Single Species and carry out the tests to the relevant minimum number of Single Species required for testing as described in condition 4.1. The minimum number of Single Species required for testing under condition 4.1 is different to the minimum number set out in condition 3.3 above, you must comply with both.
  2. (b) send the relevant test samples from the tests described in condition 4.1 to a laboratory for analysis;
  3. (c) conduct a review of the general health and welfare of the relevant Single Species;
  4. (d) provide you with the relevant test results from the tests described in condition 4.1, including the detailed laboratory analysis;
  5. (e) provide you with a written report (the “Report”) and you must ask the Vet to follow the Beta 3 guidance setting out what should be in the Report;
  6. (f) provide you with a separate review summary sheet (the “Review Summary Sheet”) as described in condition 5 to accompany the Report referred to in condition 3.4(e).

4.Testing a Single Species

4.1 You must ask the Vet to carry out one (1) of the following tests of the relevant minimum number of the relevant Single Species on the date or dates the Vet carries out the Review:

  1. (a) test at least six (6) beef cattle for Bovine Viral Diarrhoea (BVD) using a blood (serum) antibody test;
  2. (b) test any number of dairy cattle for BVD using an antibody bulk milk test or a polymerase chain reaction (PCR) bulk milk test or a blood (serum) antibody test;
  3. (c) test at least ten (10) sheep for the effectiveness of worming treatments containing one (1) of the following active chemicals:
    1. i. Benzimidazole (BZ)
    2. ii. Levamisole (LV)
    3. iii. Macrocyclic lactones (ML)
    4. iv. Amino-acetonitrile derivatives (AD)
    5. v. Spiroindoles (SI)

    6. (d) test at least thirty (30) pigs for Porcine Reproductive and Respiratory Syndrome (PRRS) using an enzyme-linked immunosorbent assay (ELISA) or PRRS PCR test.

    4.2 The tests set out in condition 4.1 are in addition to any other services which you may agree for the Vet to provide at the same time as the Review, but which are not covered by the Payment.

    4.3 You must ask the Vet to send the samples from the tests set out in condition 4.1 to a suitably accredited laboratory for the relevant analysis, the Vet guidance has further information on what a suitably accredited laboratory for each Single Species will be.

    4.4 You must ask the Vet to share the results analysis received from the laboratory explained in condition 4.3 for the relevant tests set out in condition 4.1.

    4.5 You cannot use test results you have already obtained under another accreditation scheme or any other legal or contractual obligation you are subject to in order to meet the testing requirements set out in condition 4.1.
